More About our foundation

Our Vision is to undertake a wholistic approach by mobilizing all stakeholders in an effort to save the lake from extinction.

Friends of Lake Ol-Bolossat Foundation (Flobf) is a non-governmental organization registered under the NGOs Board in Kenya, with the aim of promoting social, economic, and environmental justice through collective participation of the local communities living around Lake Ol-Bolossat. The lake is a freshwater body that hosts a rich diversity of wildlife and is a source of the Ewaso Nyiro River, which supports thriving wildlife and pastoral communities in northern Kenya and Somalia. Flobf works with community-based organizations, schools, faith-based organizations, the County government, and the private sector. The organization is managed by a board of directors responsible for policy formulation, project planning and implementation, budget preparation, and general management affairs. The board meets quarterly, and the day-to-day operations are overseen by a coordinator who implements the board's policies.

What We Do/Organization's Activities and Aims:

This involves Comprehensive Conservation and Sustainability Strategies for a Lake Ecosystem:

Create ecosystem monitoring programs that will enhance biodiversity conservation and natural resource management, while also improving land use and livelihoods.

Mobilizing the community to take action and participate in saving the lake from extinction.

Establish and promote ecotourism centers for recreational, educational and income generating activities.

Enhance fish farming within the lake.

Promote tree planting and growing for climate change mitigation and adaptation.

Promotion and of planting and use of indeginous trees and herbs for medicinal purposes.

Planting and growing fruit and indeginous trees for nutrition and wealth creation through carbon credit respectively.

Promotion of bee keeping and honey harvesting.

Advocate for the care and protection of Birdlife.

Establishing tree clubs in schools around the lake/county.

Organizing sensitization barazas for the communities living around the lake.
